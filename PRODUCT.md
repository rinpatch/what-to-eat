# Product

## Register

product

## Users

What To Eat Ah is for people in Singapore deciding what to eat on their phone, often while moving between plans, transit, work, school, or a friend-group chat. They are not researching a perfect restaurant from scratch; they want fast, credible, nearby food options surfaced through short creator clips and enough detail to choose confidently.

## Product Purpose

The product turns social food signals into a swipeable dinner decision tool. Clip cards are the decision unit, places are the canonical entity, and the session learns taste from swipes while respecting distance. Success means a user can open the app, swipe through a small deck, save a few credible options, and leave with a shortlist they can actually visit.

## Brand Personality

Local, quick, craveable, and lightly cheeky. The voice can use compact Singapore food-language cues, but the interface should stay task-first and trustworthy. It should feel like a sharp friend helping you pick dinner, not a content feed begging for attention.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic delivery-app UI, beige foodie-blog editorial styling, dark purple SaaS gradients, influencer marketplace dashboards, and a full TikTok clone. Do not let the product become a marketing landing page, an admin console, or a decorative restaurant directory. The swipe flow must stay mobile-first, physical, and decisive.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the decision. The first screen is always the deck, with filters and actions close to the thumb.
2. Make evidence glanceable. Clip, dish, venue, distance, creators, rating, and quote should answer "why this?" without making the user study the screen.
3. Preserve local flavor without clutter. Singlish and hawker energy belong in concise copy and color rhythm, not ornamental noise.
4. Keep the loop fast. Swiping, undo, save, details, and shortlist should feel immediate and recoverable.
5. Trust missing data. Nullable quote, price, and rating fields should disappear cleanly instead of leaving empty boxes or awkward labels.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, clear focus states, colorblind-safe left/right action states, and touch targets large enough for one-handed mobile use. Motion must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; swipe gestures need button fallbacks. Video should remain muted and inline, with poster fallbacks and meaningful text detail for users who cannot or do not watch clips.
